eXplainable PRS (XPRS)

Overview

XPRS is a web-based platform that can be used to explain and visualize PRS results.

This figure illustrates the overview of XPRS. Input: Genotype file, PRS scoring file, and GWAS association file. Step 1: Preprocessing: Align SNPs and map them to genes using positional mapping, combined SNP-to-gene mapping, and GWAS p-value or SNP heritability-based mapping. Step 2: Calculation: Compute Polygenic Risk Score (PRS), Gene Contribution Score (CSgene), variance of Gene Contribution Score, Attributed Value of Gene (Agene), and SNP Contribution Score (CSSNP). Step 3: Visualization: Population Level: Manhattan plot highlights significant risk genes based on variance in gene contribution scores. Individual Level: Distribution plot, gene-based Manhattan plot, and LocusZoom-like plot show individual's PRS and specific genetic contributions.
